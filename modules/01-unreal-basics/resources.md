# Module 01: Resources

A curated collection of tutorials, documentation, and learning materials for getting started with Unreal Engine 5.

---

## Official Epic Games Resources

### Your First Hour in Unreal Engine 5
- **URL**: [https://dev.epicgames.com/community/learning/courses/ZpX/your-first-hour-in-unreal-engine-5](https://dev.epicgames.com/community/learning/courses/ZpX/your-first-hour-in-unreal-engine-5)
- **Description**: Epic's own beginner course that walks you through the editor interface, basic navigation, placing objects, and understanding the core tools. This is the single best starting point for anyone new to UE5.

### Official UE5 Editor Documentation
- **URL**: [https://docs.unrealengine.com/5.4/en-US/unreal-editor-interface/](https://docs.unrealengine.com/5.4/en-US/unreal-editor-interface/)
- **Description**: The official reference for every panel, tool, and feature in the Unreal Editor. Bookmark this. You will come back to it often.

### Unreal Engine 5 Quick Start Guide
- **URL**: [https://docs.unrealengine.com/5.4/en-US/quick-start-guide-for-unreal-engine/](https://docs.unrealengine.com/5.4/en-US/quick-start-guide-for-unreal-engine/)
- **Description**: A step-by-step guide to creating your first project, placing actors, setting up lighting, and running a build.

### Unreal Learning Kit
- **URL**: Available on the Unreal Engine Marketplace (search "Unreal Learning Kit" in the Epic Games Launcher under the Marketplace tab).
- **Description**: A free sample project from Epic that includes pre-built environments, example Blueprints, and guided learning content. Great for exploring how a real project is structured.

## YouTube Channels

### Unreal Sensei
- **URL**: [https://www.youtube.com/@UnrealSensei](https://www.youtube.com/@UnrealSensei)
- **Description**: Excellent tutorials focused on environments, landscapes, and visual quality. His "Create a Game in UE5" series is particularly good for beginners. Clear explanations with a focus on making things look great quickly.

### Ryan Laley
- **URL**: [https://www.youtube.com/@RyanLaley](https://www.youtube.com/@RyanLaley)
- **Description**: In-depth UE5 tutorials covering Blueprints, C++, gameplay systems, and editor features. Ryan explains concepts thoroughly and is one of the best teachers for intermediate topics. His beginner series is a solid companion to this module.

### Matt Aspland
- **URL**: [https://www.youtube.com/@MattAspland](https://www.youtube.com/@MattAspland)
- **Description**: Short, focused tutorials on specific UE5 features. Great for quick "how do I do X" answers. His video library covers hundreds of topics from basic to advanced.

### Gorka Games
- **URL**: [https://www.youtube.com/@GorkaGames](https://www.youtube.com/@GorkaGames)
- **Description**: Beginner-friendly UE5 tutorials with a focus on game mechanics and practical projects. Good for learning by building actual game features.

## Recommended Free Marketplace Assets

When starting out, these free assets from the Unreal Marketplace can help you practice without needing to create your own art:

- **Starter Content** (included with every new project): Basic materials, meshes, particles, and sounds.
- **Infinity Blade Assets** (search the Marketplace): Free high-quality fantasy assets from Epic, perfect for tabletop-style environments.
- **Quixel Megascans** (integrated via Quixel Bridge): Photorealistic scanned assets, completely free for use in Unreal Engine.

## Tips for Using These Resources

1. **Start with "Your First Hour in UE5"** and follow along in the editor. Do not just watch.
2. **Bookmark the official docs**. Use them as a reference, not a tutorial. Read them when you need to understand a specific feature.
3. **Pick one YouTube channel** and follow a beginner series from start to finish before jumping around.
4. **Join a community** (Discord or Reddit) early. Asking questions is one of the fastest ways to learn, and seeing other people's questions teaches you things you did not know to ask.
